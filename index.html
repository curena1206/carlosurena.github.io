Overview

My work sits at one problem: most payments businesses confuse transaction volume with commercial performance. Volume goes up, leadership assumes the franchise is healthy. But underneath, pricing is leaking, margin is compressing, and the operating model isn't built to capture the value that's already flowing through the pipes.

I've spent twenty years fixing that. At Citi, Deutsche Bank, HSBC, and Mashreq, I built the pricing architecture, commercial frameworks, and governance structures that turn payment infrastructure into a disciplined, monetized franchise. The work has always been the same — find where value is being left on the table, build the system to capture it, and make sure it holds.

I work with banks, fintechs, and payment networks. In a full-time role, I run the payments P&L. As an advisor, I come in when an organization has payment volume but hasn't built the commercial infrastructure to monetize it properly.

How I Think About Payments P&L

Most payments franchises are flying blind on their own P&L. Revenue is coming in, volume is growing, and leadership assumes the business is healthy. It usually isn't. Pricing leakage, operational drag, exception costs, and governance gaps are quietly compressing margin — and nobody is looking.

I've spent twenty years looking. When I walk into an underperforming franchise, I don't start with the sales pipeline. I go straight to the operating model: pricing structure, governance framework, KPI discipline, cost-to-serve visibility. That's where the real problem always is. And that's where the fastest leverage is.

Frameworks & Tools

To demonstrate how I approach payments strategy in practice, I've built two working analytical frameworks — not slide decks, but functional tools.

The Payments Portfolio Diagnostic is a 42-question operator-grade assessment that scores a commercial payments franchise across six structural pillars: revenue architecture, growth quality, margin structure, multi-rail strategy, balance sheet, and governance. It produces a maturity score, pillar heatmap, and sequenced 90-day priorities.

The Payments Strategy Analytics Framework is a six-model system that transforms payment data into layered strategic intelligence, from transaction-level P&L diagnosis through client behavioral signals to board-level portfolio scenario modelling.
