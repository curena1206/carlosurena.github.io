Overview

Payments franchises often mistake volume growth for economic health. By the time the margin erosion is visible, it has been compressing for years. At Citi, Deutsche Bank, HSBC, and Mashreq, I built pricing governance frameworks for wire and cross-border products: corridor pricing models, exception governance, and portfolio margin monitoring. The work has always been the same — find where value is being left on the table, build the system to capture it, and make sure it holds.

I specialize in payments franchise economics: the pricing, corridor strategy, balance sheet linkage, and governance structures that determine whether a payments business produces durable margin. I work with banks, fintechs, and payment networks. In a full-time role, I run the payments P&L. As an advisor, I come in when an organization has payment volume but hasn't built the commercial infrastructure to monetize it properly.

How I Think About Payments P&L

Most payments franchises are flying blind on their own P&L. Revenue is coming in, volume is growing, and leadership assumes the business is healthy. It usually isn't. Pricing leakage, operational drag, exception costs, and governance gaps are quietly compressing margin — and nobody is looking.

When I walk into an underperforming franchise, I don't start with the sales pipeline. I go straight to the operating model: pricing structure, governance framework, KPI discipline, cost-to-serve visibility. That's where the real problem always is. And that's where the fastest leverage is.
